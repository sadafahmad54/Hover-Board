Safety

Using a Self-balancing board, like all sports, carries an inherent risk of injury, damage to property and death. You should always wear a helmet, pads and any other protective equipment to limit your risk. You should use the product within your abilities, learn to stop mount and dis-mount. This product is not legal on public Highways, byways, pavements or sidewalks; they may only be used on private property. Don't fall prey to peer pressure and take risks such as not wearing a helmet. Some may say it's not "cool" to wear a helmet or pads, but the risk of permanent injury or death is definitely not "cool".

SAFETY AND WELL-BEING

Using our boards is super-fun, but like any extreme sport takes a little time and dedication to become an expert. In our experience, 1 hour of practice will allow you to begin to feel confident. However, we would highly recommend initially only using the board inside, in a room that has sufficient space, and learn to practice getting on and off safely. This can be done with the help of a friend, or leaning against a wall for support. Thereafter, learn to make small movements forward, backwards and turn in a circle. You are responsible for your own safety, and we highly recommend the use of helmets and pads at all times to limit injuries. Avoid areas that are too steep, and never board near traffic, on public roads or pavements.
